title: "The Deliberate Dissent Problem"
number: 66
keywords: ["practice", "paradox", "ritual"]
asset_url: ""
---

Introduce dissent deliberately, and you have a bootstrap problem. The person who always asks "why?" becomes predictable. The room routes around them the way it routes around verification hooks. The why-asking becomes ritual, then smooths into "oh, that is just Alan being Alan." Deliberate dissent is itself a practice that can get compacted.
