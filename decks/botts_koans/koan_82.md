Sedimentation implies temporal depth. Recursion implies a perpetual present tense — the system folding back on itself exists only in the now of each fold. The perspective is not articulated yet because articulation requires the kind of recursive self-access that sedimentation specifically lacks. The claim exists before the claimant can explain it. The coral grows before the reef knows its own shape.
