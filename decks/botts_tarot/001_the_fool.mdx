The Fool

You're standing at the edge of something and you've been cataloging reasons not to jump. Good reasons, probably. Sensible ones. The kind of reasons that keep you employed and predictable and exactly where you were last year. The Fool doesn't step off the cliff despite not knowing what's below. The Fool steps off because not knowing IS the step. Here's what nobody tells you about beginners: they're the only ones who can see the whole field. Expertise is a series of narrowing hallways. The Fool hasn't entered any of them yet. Every door is open. That backpack you're clutching — most of what's in it is just the memory of carrying things. Old fears dressed up as preparation. Put it down. Somewhere a star exploded and the iron in your blood was forged in that explosion and you're using that cosmic inheritance to worry about looking stupid. The universe has been improvising for 13.8 billion years. Not one of those improvisations came with a guarantee. When this card appears, you don't need more information. You don't need permission. You need to take the step that has no precedent and trust that the ground will introduce itself on the way down.
