The High Priestess

She sits between two pillars — one black, one white — and she isn't choosing. That's the whole point. Some knowledge isn't acquired by reaching for it. It arrives when you stop reaching. You've read the book. You've asked the expert. You've searched for answers seven different ways. The answer you need isn't in any of those places. It's in the pause between the question and the effort, and the longer you sit in that pause, the deeper it gets. The High Priestess guards the threshold between what you know and what you know but haven't admitted yet. That dream you keep having. That gut feeling you keep overriding with logic. That sentence you almost said and then swallowed. She's holding all of it, waiting for you to stop talking long enough to hear. Water element. Moon-ruled. The ocean floor doesn't care about the waves. What matters is underneath, and underneath takes patience. There's a difference between searching for something and being quiet enough for it to find you. When this card appears, the information you need isn't out there. It's in here, behind a door you've been too busy to notice. Sit with the not-knowing. It knows more than you think.
