The Empress

A wheat field doesn't try to grow. It grows because the conditions are right — soil, water, sun, time. The Empress is those conditions. She's not the harvest. She's the reason there is one. You've been forcing things. Pushing the project, the relationship, the idea, through sheer will. The Empress says: stop trying to force the bloom. Make the ground hospitable and then get out of the way. Venus rules here, beauty and abundance, but the abundance isn't decorative. It's functional. A forest is abundant because abundance is what works. Scarcity is a strategy, but not hers. She spends freely because there's more where that came from. Four billion years of evolution on this one small planet produced a hundred million species. That's not efficient design. That's the Empress — reckless, gorgeous, generative overflow. The cushions on her throne, the wheat at her feet, the water flowing behind her — everything here says: there is enough. You are not running out. The fear of scarcity is a liar and the Empress has receipts. When this card appears, the question isn't whether something will grow. It's whether you'll let it grow weird and wild, or insist on trimming it into something you already understand. Let the garden be a garden. It knows what it's doing.
