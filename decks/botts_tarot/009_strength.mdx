Strength

A woman opens a lion's mouth with her bare hands. Not by overpowering it. By being calm enough that the lion forgets to bite. This is the most misunderstood card in the deck. People see "Strength" and think force. It's the opposite. It's the strength of not reacting. Leo energy — fire, ego, the roar — but tamed by patience, not suppression. The lion isn't defeated. It's befriended. I think about this in the context of difficult conversations. Someone comes in hot. The strong response isn't matching their heat. It's staying cool enough that the temperature in the room drops. Not cold — that's withdrawal. Cool. Present and unshaken. Sagan wrote about this kind of strength when he talked about skepticism as a kindness. You can hold someone's claim gently and still take it apart. The infinity symbol floats above her head, same as the Magician's, but the Magician's infinity is about potential. Strength's infinity is about endurance. This isn't a sprint. This is the long patience of geology. Mountains don't hurry. When this card appears, the answer is almost never "push harder." The answer is "hold steady. The lion will close its own mouth."
