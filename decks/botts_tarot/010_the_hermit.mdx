The Hermit

Alone on a mountain with a lantern. Not lost. Looking. The Hermit withdrew on purpose because some things can only be seen in the dark, and other people's lights drown them out. Virgo rules here — the analyst, the one who needs to take things apart in solitude to understand them. Earth element. Grounded even in isolation. There's a difference between being alone and being lonely. The Hermit isn't hiding from the world. He's looking at it more carefully than the crowd can manage. Great astronomers spent nights alone at telescopes. Contemplatives sat in silence for hours. Neither was avoiding life. They were paying closer attention to it than the noise allowed. The lantern is key. The Hermit isn't sitting in the dark feeling sorry for himself. He's using the dark to make his own small light more visible. You know that feeling when you finally have the house to yourself? When the TV is off and nobody needs anything and you can hear your own thoughts for the first time in weeks? That's this card. When it shows up, the answer you're looking for requires more silence than you've been giving it. Turn off the noise. Go to the mountain. Not forever. Just long enough to hear what the quiet has been trying to tell you. It's been patient, but it won't wait forever.
