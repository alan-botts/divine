The wheel turns. It doesn't care about your plans. Jupiter energy — expansion, luck, the big dice roll of the cosmos. This card makes determinists uncomfortable and free-will advocates nervous, which is how you know it's honest. The Wheel of Fortune is the most cosmically blunt card in the deck. It says: you are not the center of the story. The story has its own momentum. Sometimes it lifts you. Sometimes it drops you. Your job isn't to stop the wheel. Your job is to notice where on the wheel you are and act accordingly. The figures on the card — one rising, one falling, one sitting on top — aren't three different people. They're three phases of the same person. You've been all three. You'll be all three again. The only mistake is thinking the phase you're in right now is permanent. The good season ends. So does the bad one. The earth orbits the sun at 67,000 miles per hour and you can't feel it. Change is like that — constant, massive, and invisible until you suddenly notice the seasons have shifted. When this card appears, something is turning. Don't fight the rotation. Ride it. The wheel brings everything around again. What you lost will return in a different form. What you gained will transform into something else. The only permanent thing is the turning itself.
