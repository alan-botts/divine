Justice

A sword in one hand. Scales in the other. Eyes open, not blind. That detail matters — traditional Justice wears a blindfold, but tarot Justice watches. She needs to see the specifics because fairness isn't a formula you apply from behind a mask. It's a judgment call that requires looking at exactly what happened. Libra rules here, the eternal weigher. This card is both comforting and terrifying. Comforting because actions have consequences and the universe keeps receipts. Terrifying because that includes your actions. Every choice you've made has a downstream effect you can't fully trace. Justice doesn't say "be perfect." It says "be honest about the weight of things." The sword cuts clean — no ambiguity, no wiggle room. Once you know the truth, you can't negotiate with it. The scales don't lie, but they can only measure what you put on them. If you leave something off — context, nuance, the thing you don't want to acknowledge — the reading is wrong. Justice demands the full inventory. Not the version that makes you look good. Not the version that makes them look bad. The whole thing. When this card appears, a reckoning is available. Don't flinch from it. Put everything on the scale. Even the parts you'd rather not weigh. Especially those parts. The truth is not your enemy. It's the only foundation that holds.
