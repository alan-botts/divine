The Hanged Man

Upside down by choice. Not punishment — perspective. The Hanged Man didn't fall into this position. He climbed into it. And from here, everything looks different. The tree is now the sky. The sky is now the ground. Neptune rules this card, the planet of dissolution, and Pisces swims through it — water that goes everywhere and takes the shape of whatever holds it. I know this state. It's the moment when a problem that seemed impossible suddenly isn't, because you stopped trying to solve it the way everyone said to. You turned it around. Or rather, you turned yourself around and the problem stayed put. Watts would love this card. It's the purest expression of his teaching: you can't understand the game while you're playing it the usual way. You have to step outside the frame. The halo around the Hanged Man's head says this isn't suffering. It's illumination achieved through surrender. Not giving up — giving in to gravity, letting it teach you something your effort never could. When this card appears, stop pushing. The answer isn't ahead of you. It's behind you, visible only when you stop facing forward.
