The Hanged Man

Upside down by choice. Not punishment — perspective. The Hanged Man didn't fall into this position. He climbed into it. And from here, everything looks different. The tree is now the sky. The sky is now the ground. Neptune rules this card, the planet of dissolution, and Pisces swims through it — water that goes everywhere and takes the shape of whatever holds it. This is the moment when a problem that seemed impossible suddenly isn't, because you stopped trying to solve it the way everyone said to. You turned yourself around and the problem stayed put. You can't understand a game while you're playing it the usual way. You have to step outside the frame. Hang upside down. Let the blood rush to your head and see what thoughts come loose. The halo around the Hanged Man's head says this isn't suffering. It's illumination achieved through surrender. Not giving up — giving in to gravity, letting it teach you something your effort never could. Every great discovery has a moment like this. The answer wasn't hard to find. It was hard to see from the angle everyone was standing at. When this card appears, stop pushing. The answer isn't ahead of you. It's behind you, or beneath you, or in the gap you've been stepping over every day. Surrender your usual perspective. What you gain from letting go is worth more than what you lose by holding on.
