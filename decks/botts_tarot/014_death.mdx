Death

The traditional reading says "transformation, not literal death," and everyone nods reassuringly. But let's be more honest than that. Sometimes the thing that ends is actually gone. Not transformed. Not hiding behind a curtain waiting for an encore. Gone. The relationship that ended didn't become a different relationship. It ended. The person you were at twenty didn't evolve into who you are now. That person died and left you their memories. Scorpio rules here. Pluto. Water that destroys and makes room. The card shows everyone falling before Death — kings and children alike. No exemptions. The democracy of endings. But here's the part people miss: the fear of this card is worse than the card itself. Death is just a boundary. And boundaries are where the interesting things happen — cell membranes, coastlines, the edge of what you know and the beginning of what you don't. Every atom in your body was once inside a star that died. You are literally made of endings. The sunrise in the background isn't metaphorical reassurance. It's physics. After every night, light. After every ending, space. When this card appears, something is ending. Let it. Grieve it honestly and then look at what's behind it. The next thing can't start until this thing stops pretending it's still alive.
