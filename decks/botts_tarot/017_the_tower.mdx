The Tower

Lightning hits. The crown flies off. Bodies fall. Everyone hates drawing this card, which is exactly why it's important. The Tower is the thing you built on a bad foundation finally collapsing, and the collapse is a mercy even though it doesn't feel like one. Mars energy — sudden, violent, transformative. Fire that doesn't warm. Fire that clears. You weren't going to voluntarily tear down the thing you spent so long building. You needed the lightning. The marriage that looked perfect from outside. The career that everyone envied. The belief about yourself that organized your whole life. When the lightning hits, the thing that falls was already hollow. The Tower doesn't destroy truth. It destroys everything that isn't true, and what's left standing after the fire is the only thing that was ever real. The figures falling from the Tower aren't dying. They're being evicted from a structure that was going to kill them if they stayed. That's the gift inside the disaster. You never would have left on your own. When this card appears, something is breaking. Let it break. You'll stand in the rubble feeling stunned and exposed and free in a way that terrifies you. The thing that replaces the tower will have better foundations, because now you know what bad ones look like.
