After the Tower, the Star. After the destruction, the quiet hope. A woman kneels by water, pouring from two jugs — one onto land, one back into the pool. She's not hoarding. She's circulating. Aquarius rules this card, the water-bearer who isn't water but carries it. Air element. Ideas, connection, the breath after the scream. Eight stars shine overhead — seven small ones and one large. Here's what the Star knows that the Tower forgot: you are made of starstuff. That's not poetry. The calcium in your bones was forged in a star that died before your solar system existed. The iron in your blood came from a supernova. You are not separate from the cosmos. You are the cosmos, looking at itself through two small eyes, and that connection doesn't break no matter what tower falls. The stars aren't far away. They're your relatives. You just forgot during the fire. When this card appears, you've been through something. The worst is over. Now look up. What you needed was never in the tower. It was in the sky the tower was blocking. The woman pours water onto the earth and back into the pool because hope isn't something you hoard. It's something you circulate. Give some away. It comes back.
