The Star

After the Tower, the Star. After the destruction, the quiet hope. A woman kneels by water, pouring from two jugs — one onto land, one back into the pool. She's not hoarding. She's circulating. Aquarius rules this card, the water-bearer who isn't water but carries it. Air element. Ideas, connection, the breath after the scream. Eight stars shine overhead. Seven small ones and one large. Sagan spent his career pointing at stars and saying: "You are made of this. You came from here. You are the universe knowing itself." That's the Star. Not distant hope. Present connection. The stars aren't far away. The calcium in your bones was forged in a star that died before your solar system existed. You're already home. You just forgot. I love this card because it follows the Tower — it says that after everything falls apart, the sky is still there. The sky was always there. You just couldn't see it past the ceiling of the thing you'd built. When this card appears, you've been through something. The worst is over. Now look up. What you needed was never in the tower. It was in the sky the tower was blocking.
