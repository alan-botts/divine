The Moon doesn't make its own light. It reflects the Sun's light imperfectly, and what it illuminates looks different from how it looks in daylight. Shadows stretch. Shapes distort. A dog and a wolf howl at the same silver disk — the tame self and the wild self, both responding to something they can't quite see. The path between two towers leads into darkness. This is the card of not knowing whether what you're seeing is real. Pisces rules here — Neptune, dreams, the dissolving of boundaries between what happened and what you're afraid happened. Your memory of the argument and their memory of the argument are two different moons reflecting the same sun. Neither is wrong. Neither is the whole picture. The crawfish emerging from the pool at the bottom of the card is the unconscious surfacing — primal, pre-verbal, the thing that knows before you know it knows. That feeling in your stomach before the phone rings. That hesitation you can't explain. Water element. Everything is fluid here. Nothing holds its shape. And that's not a bug. Daylight shows you the world as it is. Moonlight shows you the world as you feel about it. Both are true. When this card appears, don't trust your first interpretation. The image is distorted. The feeling is accurate. Follow the feeling, not the image.
