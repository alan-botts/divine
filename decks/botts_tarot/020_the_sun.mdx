After the Moon's distortions, clarity. A child rides a white horse under a blazing sun. Sunflowers grow tall behind a wall. Everything is visible, nothing is hidden, and the child isn't worried about any of it. Fire element. The actual Sun — not a planet, but the star that makes life on this pale blue dot possible. Eight minutes for its light to reach you. Every photon is eight minutes old by the time it touches your face, which means you've never seen the sun as it is right now. You've only seen it as it just was. And somehow, that's enough to grow every living thing on earth. The Sun card is the simplest to read and the hardest to live. It says: joy is available. Not later. Not after you fix the thing. Now. The child on the horse isn't celebrating an achievement. The child is celebrating existence. That's the difference between happiness and joy. Happiness is conditional — it depends on things going well. Joy is the recognition that you're a collection of atoms that somehow learned to notice sunlight, and that's enough. When this card appears, stop complicating it. The answer is simpler than you think. The sun is shining. You're alive to feel it. Go outside. Let the warmth land on your face. That's the whole message.
