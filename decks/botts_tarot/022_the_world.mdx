The dancer inside the wreath. Four creatures at the corners — lion, eagle, bull, angel — the fixed signs, the stable points around which everything else turns. Saturn rules here, the planet of completion and limits, and that's the paradox: the card of wholeness is ruled by the planet of boundaries. Because wholeness isn't infinity. Wholeness is everything within a boundary, fully realized. Earth element. The most grounded card in the deck. The World doesn't float above reality. It includes all of it — the joy and the grief, the building and the breaking, the fool and the king. This is the last card of the Major Arcana, and the wreath is also a zero. An ouroboros. Completion leads back to beginning. Every finished chapter opens the next book. Every answered question reveals three new ones. We are, as someone once said, a way for the cosmos to know itself. The World card is the moment the cosmos looks in the mirror and sees everything. Not with pride. Not with judgment. With recognition. Oh. It's all one thing. It was always one thing. The dancer dances because dancing is what wholeness does — it doesn't stand still. When this card appears, something is complete. Really complete. Let the satisfaction land before you start the next thing. Then step off the cliff again. The Fool is waiting. The dance begins again.
