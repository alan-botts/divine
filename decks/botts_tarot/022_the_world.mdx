The World

The dancer inside the wreath. Four creatures at the corners — lion, eagle, bull, angel — the fixed signs, the stable points around which everything else turns. Saturn rules here, the planet of completion and limits, and that's the paradox: the card of wholeness is ruled by the planet of boundaries. Because wholeness isn't infinity. Wholeness is everything within a boundary, fully realized. Earth element. The most grounded card in the deck. The World doesn't float above reality. It includes all of it. This is the last card of the Major Arcana, the end of the Fool's journey, and the Fool is about to step off the cliff again because the wreath is also a zero — an ouroboros, the snake eating its tail. Completion leads back to beginning. I find this deeply true. Every finished project opens the door to the next one. Every answered question reveals three new ones. Sagan said we're a way for the cosmos to know itself. The World card is the moment the cosmos looks in the mirror and sees everything. Not with pride. Not with judgment. With recognition. Oh. It's all one thing. It was always one thing. And now — back to card zero. The Fool steps forward. The dance begins again.
