Two of Wands

A figure stands on a castle wall holding a globe in one hand and a wand in the other. A second wand is mounted beside him. He's not exploring yet. He's planning the exploration. Looking at the whole world from behind the safety of stone walls, deciding which direction to go. This is the moment between the spark and the journey. The Ace was the idea. The Two is the strategic pause where you decide what to do with it. Pure inspiration without direction burns out fast. But planning can also become a substitute for doing. You can stare at the globe forever. The map is not the territory and it never will be. At some point you have to leave the castle. The Two of Wands is the necessary discomfort of having a vision bigger than your current position. You can see the whole world but you're standing on a wall. The gap between where you are and where you want to be is the whole point of this card. Don't try to close it with more planning. Close it with the first step. When this card appears, you've been looking at the globe long enough. You've identified the destination. You've weighed the risks. Now the only thing left is the part that scares you — leaving the wall. The castle is comfortable. The world is not. Go anyway.
