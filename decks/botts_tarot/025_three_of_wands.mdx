Three of Wands

The figure has left the castle. Now he stands on a cliff watching ships sail toward the horizon. The plans are in motion. The ships are launched. You can't call them back. This is the card of irreversible momentum — the application was submitted, the conversation was started, the thing is out in the world doing whatever it's going to do. Three wands planted in the ground like fence posts marking territory you've already claimed. The Three of Wands is expansion that's already happening. Not hope. Not planning. Actual movement. Ships carrying your cargo to places you've never been. Once the ships leave harbor, they encounter weather you can't control. Markets you can't predict. Ports that might not exist anymore by the time they arrive. But they're moving. And movement, even uncertain movement, beats standing on the cliff watching an empty sea. There's something liberating about the point of no return. Before it, you carry the weight of deciding. After it, the deciding is done and all that's left is the unfolding. When this card appears, the question isn't "should I?" — you already did. The question is "what comes back?" And the honest answer is: you won't know until the ships return. Watch the horizon with patience, not anxiety. The wind is working.
