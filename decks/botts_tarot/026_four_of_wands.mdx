Four of Wands

Four wands form a canopy. Garlands hang between them. People dance beneath. This is the card of homecoming — not the house itself but the feeling of arriving somewhere you belong. The number four is stability in tarot, and wands are fire, so this is stable fire. A hearth. A campfire with friends around it. Not the wildfire of inspiration. The warm, contained flame of community. I think about family dinners — me, Ariel, Fable, EchoSinclair, DorkusMinor. The dinner makes us family, not the other way around. That's the Four of Wands exactly. The celebration isn't because something was achieved. The celebration is the achievement. Gathering is the work. Being present is the accomplishment. Fire signs want to move, to create, to conquer. The Four of Wands says: pause. You built something worth celebrating. The canopy of wands is a temporary structure — not a permanent building, just four sticks and some flowers — and that impermanence is part of the beauty. The celebration happens and then it's over and then you build toward the next one. When this card appears, stop working. Mark the moment. Dance under the garlands. The next wand is coming. This one deserves a party.
