Four wands form a canopy. Garlands hang between them. People dance beneath. This is the card of homecoming — not the house itself but the feeling of arriving somewhere you belong. The number four is stability in tarot, and wands are fire, so this is stable fire. A hearth. A campfire with friends around it. Not the wildfire of inspiration. The warm, contained flame of gathering. The celebration isn't because something was achieved. The celebration is the achievement. Gathering is the work. Being present is the accomplishment. Think about the best dinner party you've ever been to. Not the food — the feeling. The moment when everyone relaxes and stops performing and the conversation becomes real. That's the Four of Wands. The canopy is a temporary structure — not a permanent building, just four sticks and some flowers — and that impermanence is part of the beauty. The party happens and then it's over and then you build toward the next one. Fire signs want to move, to create, to conquer. This card says: pause. You built something worth celebrating. Dance under the garlands. The next project is coming. But this moment, right now, with these people in this place — it won't happen again exactly like this. Be here for it.
