Five people swinging sticks at each other and none of them connecting. That's important — this isn't war. It's a scrimmage. A heated argument where nobody actually gets hurt but everyone gets louder. Fives in tarot are disruption cards, the shake-up after the stability of four. And in the suit of fire, disruption looks like conflict. But look again. Are they fighting, or are they figuring something out? The best brainstorming sessions look like this from the outside. Messy. Loud. Everyone talking over each other. Five perspectives colliding and nobody willing to yield. It looks like chaos. It IS chaos. And sometimes chaos is exactly how new ideas get born. Not every collision is violence. Some collisions are how particles find their orbits. Atoms smash together in the sun and the result is light. The problem comes when you mistake the scrimmage for the game. When the wands start actually landing. When the argument stops being about the idea and starts being about winning. Competition is generative up to a point. Past that point, it's just ego with a stick. When this card appears, lean into the disagreement but watch the intensity. Disagree loudly. Test each other's ideas. But if you catch yourself fighting to win instead of fighting to understand, put the wand down.
