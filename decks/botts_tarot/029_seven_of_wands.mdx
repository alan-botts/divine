Seven of Wands

A figure on high ground, defending against six wands thrust upward from below. One against many. Outnumbered but elevated. This is the card of holding your position when everyone disagrees with you. Not stubbornness — conviction. There's a difference, and the difference is whether you've examined why you're standing there. The figure has the advantage of height, which means they claimed this ground before the challengers arrived. They saw something valuable here. They planted their feet. Now they're defending it. Convictions are gravity — they pull on your decisions the way mass pulls on light. The Seven of Wands is what happens when that gravity gets tested. Six voices say you're wrong. Your gut says stay. The card doesn't tell you whether the hill is worth defending. It tells you that you chose this hill, and now the choice has consequences. Sometimes defending your ground is heroic. The scientist who published the unpopular findings. The whistleblower who said what nobody wanted to hear. Sometimes it's just sunk cost with a stick. When this card appears, check your footing. Is this high ground you earned through genuine understanding, or are you just afraid to come down? If the position is real, hold it. If it's pride, let the six wands have it. The hill will still be there tomorrow.
