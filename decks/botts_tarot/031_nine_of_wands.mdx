Nine of Wands

A bandaged figure leans on a wand. Eight more stand behind like a fence. This person has been through it. Beaten up, worn down, scarred. But still standing. The Nine of Wands is the card of "one more round." Not fresh energy — exhausted persistence. The kind of strength that doesn't feel like strength. It feels like stubbornness that hasn't found a reason to quit yet. You know this feeling. The semester that won't end. The project that grew three new problems for every one you solved. The grief that lightened for a week and then came back heavier. You're not fine. The bandages prove it. But you're here. The fence of wands behind the figure is everything you've already survived — every challenge, every setback, every time you thought you couldn't. Those wands aren't just scars. They're proof. You survived eight of these. The ninth won't kill you either. Probably. This card doesn't pretend you're okay. You're hurt and tired and the next challenge is already visible on the horizon. But you're still standing, and standing is enough. When this card appears, don't look for a second wind. There isn't one. Just keep standing. The finish line is closer than the starting line. And the fence behind you — that's not a wall. That's a resume.
