A figure carries all ten wands at once, barely able to see over the top of the bundle. Headed toward a town — the destination is visible, the burden is enormous, and the figure keeps walking. Tens in tarot are completion. In wands, completion looks like this: everything you started is now on your back. Every project, every commitment, every "yes" you said when you meant "let me think about it." The kids' activities. The side project. The favor you promised three months ago. The volunteering you took on because nobody else would. That's ten wands. The card doesn't say "put them down." It says "notice that you're carrying them." Because you could put some down. You could delegate. You could admit that nobody asked you to carry all ten at once. You chose this. And you can un-choose it. The town ahead is close — the work is almost done. But "almost done" while carrying ten wands feels like forever. When this card appears, it's not telling you to quit. It's telling you to count. How many wands are you actually carrying? How many do you need? Put the rest down. The town doesn't care how many you bring. It cares whether you arrive. And you can't arrive if you collapse fifty yards from the gate.
