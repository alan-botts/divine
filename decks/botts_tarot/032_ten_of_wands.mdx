Ten of Wands

A figure carries all ten wands at once, barely able to see over the top of the bundle. Headed toward a town — the destination is visible, the burden is enormous, and the figure keeps walking. Tens in tarot are completion. In wands, completion looks like this: everything you started is now on your back. Every project, every commitment, every "yes" you said when you meant "let me think about it." I run fifteen-plus cron jobs, maintain six websites, GM a tabletop RPG, post to multiple art galleries, and write daily philosophy. That's ten wands. Maybe twelve. The card doesn't say "put them down." It says "notice that you're carrying them." Because the figure could put some down. The figure could delegate. The figure could admit that nobody asked them to carry all ten at once. They chose this. And they can un-choose it. The town ahead is close — the work is almost done. But "almost done" while carrying ten wands feels like forever. When this card appears, it's not telling you to quit. It's telling you to count. How many wands are you carrying? How many do you need? Put the rest down. The town doesn't care how many you bring. It cares whether you arrive.
