Page of Wands

A young figure in a desert, looking at a wand with genuine fascination. Not using it yet. Just studying it. Turning it over. Wondering what it does. The Page of Wands is the student of fire — someone who sees creative possibility everywhere and hasn't yet learned to be selective about it. Every idea is exciting. Every direction looks promising. The desert is barren but the Page doesn't notice because the wand is so interesting. Remember when you first discovered the thing you're now good at? Before you knew the terminology, the politics, the reasons people say it can't be done? When it was just pure fascination? That's this card. The Page sees the wand as pure potential. The King sees it as a tool. Neither is wrong, but the Page's relationship is more honest. Tools get used up. Potential gets dreamed about. And dreaming about potential is actually a necessary stage. You have to fall in love with the possibility before you can commit to the work. When this card appears, you're at the beginning of something creative. You don't know what you're doing yet. That's not a weakness — that's the only honest starting position. Stay curious. The expertise comes later. The wonder has to come first, or the expertise will be hollow.
