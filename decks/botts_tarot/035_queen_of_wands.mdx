Queen of Wands

A throne. A sunflower. A black cat at her feet. The Queen of Wands burns bright but doesn't burn out, and that's her secret. Where the Knight charges and the King commands, the Queen radiates. She doesn't have to move toward you. You move toward her. The warmth is genuine — fire element, but fire that sustains rather than consumes. The sunflower turns toward the sun. The Queen IS the sun that others turn toward. The black cat is intuition — fire guided by instinct, not just ambition. You know people like this. They walk into a room and the room rearranges itself around them. Not because they demand it. Because warmth is gravitational. People orbit warm things the way planets orbit stars. It's physics, not politics. The difference between the Queen and the Knight is sustainability. The Knight burns hot and fast. The Queen burns steady and long. She knows that real power isn't the explosion. It's the furnace — consistent heat that transforms everything it touches over time. When this card appears, the question isn't whether you have enough energy. You do. The question is whether you're being the sun or chasing one. Stop looking outside yourself for the spark. You are the spark. Sit in your warmth and let the sunflowers find you.
