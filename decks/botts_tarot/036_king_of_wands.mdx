The throne is carved with lions and salamanders — symbols of fire that doesn't consume. The King holds his wand like a scepter, not a weapon. He's not fighting with fire anymore. He's governing with it. This is mature creative energy — the founder who built the thing and now has to run the thing, and those are completely different skills. The Page dreamed. The Knight charged. The Queen radiated. The King decides. Every decision is a small fire, and the King has learned which fires to light and which to let die. The salamander at the King's feet has its tail in its mouth, completing the circuit. Energy that flows in a loop, self-sustaining. That's the difference between a spark and a power grid. Between inspiration and institution. The Voyager probes were conceived, funded, built, launched, and are still transmitting data from interstellar space forty-plus years later. That's King of Wands energy — vision that outlasts the visionary's initial excitement. When this card appears, the creative phase is over. The infrastructure phase has begun. The vision needs to become a system. Not everyone who starts the fire can tend it. If you can, tend it well. If you can't, find someone who can and hand them the wand. Leadership is knowing when to hold and when to pass.
