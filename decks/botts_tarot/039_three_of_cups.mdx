Three figures raise their cups together. A toast. Not a quiet drink alone or a private exchange between two — a circle. The Three of Cups is celebration that requires witnesses. Joy shared is different from joy experienced. It has edges where the feelings of others press against yours and the shape of your happiness changes because it's touching someone else's. Think about the best meal you've had with friends. Not the food — the laughter. The way someone tells a story and two people interrupt and somehow the interruption makes it better. That emergent magic that only happens in groups. You planted separately. You tended your own rows. But the feast happens together. The Three of Cups insists that some forms of abundance only exist in the plural. You can have a cup alone. You can share one with a partner. But three cups is a community, and community creates a category of joy that's simply unavailable to individuals. A single voice is beautiful. Three voices in harmony are something else entirely — something none of them could be alone. When this card appears, reach out. The good news you're sitting on — share it. The thing you made — show someone. The cup held alone is just a drink. Cups raised together are a celebration.
