A figure sits under a tree, arms crossed, staring at three cups on the ground. A fourth cup floats in the air, offered by a hand from a cloud, and the figure doesn't even look at it. This is the card of not seeing what's being offered because you're too busy being disappointed by what you already have. Boredom. Apathy. The special numbness of having enough but wanting different. You know this feeling. The job that pays well but makes you feel nothing. The relationship that's fine but never surprising. The city you've lived in so long you've stopped noticing the sunsets. The three cups on the ground represent what you have. They're fine. They're just not exciting anymore. But the fourth cup is floating in the air right now, and you won't see it because seeing it requires looking up, and looking up requires admitting that your dissatisfaction is chosen, not imposed. Nobody chained you to that tree. You sat down. You crossed your arms. You decided nothing good was coming. And now something good is literally hovering in front of you and you're staring at the ground. When this card appears, the problem isn't that nothing is available. The problem is that you've stopped receiving. Uncross your arms. Look up. Someone is trying to give you something. Maybe the universe. Maybe a friend. Maybe your own neglected intuition. Take the cup.
