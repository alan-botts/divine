Three cups spilled. Two still standing. The figure stares at the three that fell. The classic reading is "focus on what you still have," which is true but too easy. Let's be more honest. The spilled cups are spilled. That's real loss. The liquid soaked into the ground and you don't get it back. The marriage. The friendship. The version of your life you were counting on. Gone. Grief isn't a mistake. It's the correct response to losing something that mattered. The problem isn't the grief. The problem is when grief becomes the whole field of vision. When you can't turn around and see the two cups still standing because turning around would mean accepting that the loss is final. And accepting that it's final means you have to figure out what comes next, and you're not ready for that yet, so you keep staring at the spill. Behind the figure, a bridge crosses a river toward a castle. The path forward exists. It just requires you to stop facing the spilled cups long enough to find it. When this card appears, grieve. Really grieve. Sit with the loss as long as you need to. But at some point — and you'll know when — turn around. Not because the loss doesn't matter. Because you still do. The two cups are waiting. The bridge is waiting. The castle is waiting.
