A child offers a cup full of flowers to another child. Six cups total, all blooming. The scene is a garden, walled and safe. This is the card of nostalgia — the sweet ache of looking backward at something simpler. The smell of your grandmother's kitchen. The neighborhood you grew up in, smaller now than you remember. The friend you lost touch with who still shows up in dreams. The innocence in this card isn't childish. It's pre-complicated. Before you learned all the reasons to be careful, before the walls went up, before you started editing yourself mid-sentence. The child offers the flowers without calculating the response. The gift is pure because the giver hasn't learned to be strategic yet. You used to be like that. You used to say what you meant and want what you wanted and the distance between feeling and expression was zero. You can't go back to that. The garden is walled for a reason — it's a memory, not a destination. But you can honor it. You can let it remind you of what you were like before you got so careful. When this card appears, something from the past is relevant. Not as a place to return to — time only flows one direction. But as a compass pointing toward something you forgot you needed. The simplicity was real. Let it teach you something about the complexity you're living in now.
