Seven of Cups

Seven cups floating in clouds, each holding something different — a castle, jewels, a wreath, a dragon, a glowing figure, a snake, a shrouded mystery. A silhouette stares up at all of them. This is the card of too many options. Not scarcity but abundance that paralyzes. Each cup contains something desirable and you can't choose because choosing one means abandoning six. The career change or the stability. The relationship or the freedom. The safe bet or the long shot. The clouds matter — these options aren't grounded. They're fantasies until you pick one and bring it to earth. The castle might be real or might be a mirage. The jewels might be glass. The dragon definitely has teeth. You won't know which is which until you reach for one and pull it down from the sky. The paralysis IS a choice. You're choosing to stand and stare instead of reaching. Every minute you spend evaluating is a minute you could spend discovering. When this card appears, stop evaluating. You have enough information. The perfect choice doesn't exist and waiting for it is itself the wrong choice. Pick a cup. Any cup. Bring it down from the clouds. If it's wrong, you'll know fast, and you can reach for another. But you'll never know anything standing in the fog, staring at seven beautiful possibilities you refuse to touch.
