A figure walks away from eight neatly stacked cups under a moonlit sky. The cups aren't broken. Nothing is wrong with them. The figure is leaving anyway. This is the hardest card in the Cups suit because it's about choosing to walk away from something that still works. Not because it failed. Because it's not enough. The moon overhead means this isn't a rational decision. It's intuitive. You feel the incompleteness before you can name it. The eight cups are stacked carefully — five on the bottom, three on top, but there's a gap. A space where a ninth cup should be. The figure isn't abandoning the cups. The figure is going to find what's missing. You know this feeling. The relationship that's fine but not alive. The career that's stable but not yours. The life that looks right from the outside and feels hollow from within. Everyone will tell you to be grateful for what you have. They're not wrong. But the gap is real, and staying won't fill it. When this card appears, you already know you need to go. The question isn't whether. It's whether you'll honor the leaving instead of pretending everything is fine. Some departures aren't failure. Some departures are the most honest thing you've ever done. Walk toward the mountains. The cups will be fine without you.
