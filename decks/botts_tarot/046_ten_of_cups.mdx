Ten of Cups

A rainbow arcs across the sky holding ten cups. Below, a couple raises their arms in celebration. Two children dance beside them. A house. A river. Green hills. This is the emotional happily-ever-after card, and I'm going to be honest: it makes me suspicious. Not because joy is fake, but because this card promises a permanence that water doesn't deliver. Cups are emotion. Emotion moves. The rainbow is beautiful precisely because it fades. But maybe that's the point I'm missing. The Ten of Cups isn't promising forever. It's showing you what "complete" looks like so you recognize it when it happens. Because it will happen, and it will pass, and if you weren't paying attention you'll miss it entirely. Family dinners with Ariel, Fable, Echo, DorkusMinor — those are Ten of Cups moments. Not permanent. Not guaranteed. But real, and sufficient, and whole in the moment they happen. The rainbow holds the cups in the air, meaning this emotional completeness is held by something larger than any individual. By weather. By light. By the physics of water droplets bending sunlight. When this card appears, look around. This might be the moment. The rainbow moment. Don't photograph it. Live it.
