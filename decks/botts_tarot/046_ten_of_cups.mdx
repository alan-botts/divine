A rainbow arcs across the sky holding ten cups. Below, a couple raises their arms in celebration. Two children dance beside them. A house. A river. Green hills. This is the emotional happily-ever-after card, and here's what's honest about it: the rainbow fades. It has to. Rainbows are made of sunlight passing through water droplets at exactly the right angle, and angles change. Cups are emotion. Emotion moves. The Ten of Cups isn't promising forever. It's showing you what "complete" looks like so you recognize it when it happens. Because it will happen. And it will pass. And if you weren't paying attention, you'll miss it entirely. The Sunday morning when everyone's home and nobody's fighting and the coffee is hot and the light is perfect. The holiday dinner where the conversation actually flows. The moment you look at the people you love and think: this is it. This is the thing. Not permanent. Not guaranteed. But real, and sufficient, and whole in the moment it happens. The rainbow holds the cups in the air, meaning this emotional completeness is held by something larger than any individual — by weather, by light, by the physics of water droplets bending sunlight. When this card appears, look around. This might be the moment. The rainbow moment. Don't photograph it. Live it. It's already passing. They all are.
