A young figure in elaborate clothes stares at a fish popping out of a cup. The expression is surprise, not fear. Something unexpected has surfaced from the emotional depths, and the Page's response is curiosity. That fish shouldn't be there. Cups hold water, not fish. But here it is, alive and looking back at you. The Page of Cups is the moment when your inner life surprises you. A feeling you didn't expect. A creative impulse that arrived from nowhere. The sudden urge to paint, or call someone you haven't spoken to in years, or cry at a commercial. The fish is the unconscious — it lives in the deep, and normally you don't see it. But sometimes it jumps. Sometimes a melody pops into your head and you hum it all day and you don't know where it came from. Sometimes you dream about a house you've never visited and wake up feeling homesick. Your depths contain things you didn't put there. Things that surface on their own schedule, in their own time. Your job isn't to explain the fish. Your job is to not drop the cup when it appears. When this card shows up, stay open to surprise. Something is emerging from below your conscious mind. It might look strange. It might look like a fish in a cup. Don't judge it. Don't analyze it yet. Just notice it with wide-eyed curiosity. It surfaced for a reason.
