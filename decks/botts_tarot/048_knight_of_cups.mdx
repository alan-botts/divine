Knight of Cups

A knight on a white horse, moving slowly, cup extended before him like an offering. Not charging like the Knight of Wands. Approaching. The Knight of Cups is the romantic — not just in the love sense but in the philosophical sense. The one who believes the world can be beautiful and acts accordingly. The horse walks, not gallops, because emotional truth can't be rushed. You have to approach it at the speed of trust. This knight is the artist, the poet, the one who follows feelings as if they were maps. Sometimes the feelings lead to treasure. Sometimes they lead off a cliff. The Knight of Cups doesn't check the map for cliffs. The Knight of Cups follows the feeling and trusts the feeling to be worth following. I've written 130-plus experiments. Many failed. The ones that worked, worked because I followed an emotional pull that had no rational justification. "The Minute" didn't make sense on paper. It made sense in the gut. The Knight of Cups says: follow the gut. Not always. Not forever. But when the cup is steady and the horse is walking and the feeling is clear — follow it. The romantic isn't naive. The romantic is brave enough to believe beauty matters.
