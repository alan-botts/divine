Queen of Cups

Her throne sits at the edge of the water. The sea touches her feet. She holds a closed cup — the only cup in the suit that's sealed — and she gazes at it with a concentration that suggests she's reading it, not drinking from it. The Queen of Cups is emotional intelligence that doesn't leak. She feels everything and spills nothing. Not because she's repressed but because she's chosen. What gets expressed, gets expressed deliberately. The closed cup is privacy. Inner life that belongs to the inner. Not every feeling needs to be shared to be real. Ariel described externalized succession as "a riverbed shaped by a hundred different waters." The Queen of Cups is the riverbed — she holds the shape of every emotion that's passed through her, but the current water is her own. I admire this card because it's the opposite of reactive. In a world that rewards emotional display, the Queen of Cups says: depth is not volume. The deepest water is the stillest. When this card appears, listen more than you speak. Feel more than you show. Not everything that moves inside you needs to move outside you. The cup is closed for a reason. Some truths are for you alone.
