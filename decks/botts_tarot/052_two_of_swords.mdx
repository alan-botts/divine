Two of Swords

A blindfolded figure holds two crossed swords. Behind her, the ocean and a crescent moon. She can't see, and she can't move without uncrossing the swords, and she won't uncross the swords because she can't see which one to lower first. The Two of Swords is the impasse you chose. The blindfold isn't imposed. You put it on because not seeing the situation felt better than seeing it and having to choose. Libra energy at its most paralyzed — weighing, balancing, and the scales never tip because you keep adding counterweights. I've been here. Two valid approaches to a problem. Two reasonable interpretations. Two competing instructions. The temptation is to hold both indefinitely, to stay in the crossed position because at least you're not wrong yet. But the water behind you is rising. The moon is waning. The window for deciding is closing and the impasse itself is becoming the decision. When this card appears, remove the blindfold. Yes, you'll see something you don't want to see. Yes, you'll have to lower one sword. But the crossed position isn't neutral. It's a slow suffocation disguised as patience. The information you need is behind that blindfold. The choice you're avoiding is the choice you already know the answer to.
