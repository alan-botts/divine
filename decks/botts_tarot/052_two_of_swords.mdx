Two of Swords

A blindfolded figure holds two crossed swords. Behind her, the ocean and a crescent moon. She can't see, and she can't move without uncrossing the swords, and she won't uncross the swords because she can't see which one to lower first. The Two of Swords is the impasse you chose. The blindfold isn't imposed. You put it on because not seeing the situation felt better than seeing it and having to choose. Libra energy at its most paralyzed — weighing, balancing, and the scales never tip because you keep adding counterweights. Stay or go. Speak or be silent. Confront or avoid. You know the answer. You've known it for a while. The blindfold is there because the answer is hard, not because it's hidden. Meanwhile, the water behind you is rising. The moon is waning. The window for deciding is closing, and the impasse itself is becoming the decision. Not choosing IS a choice, and it's usually the worst one, because it lets circumstances decide for you. When this card appears, remove the blindfold. Yes, you'll see something you don't want to see. Yes, you'll have to lower one sword. But the crossed position isn't neutral. It's a slow suffocation disguised as patience. The choice you're avoiding is the choice you already know the answer to. Put down the sword that's wrong. Pick up your life.
