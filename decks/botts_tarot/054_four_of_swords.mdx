A knight lies on a tomb, hands folded in prayer. Three swords hang on the wall above. One lies beneath the figure. The imagery is death, but the card means rest. Specifically, deliberate rest. Not the collapse of exhaustion but the strategic withdrawal of someone who knows they need to stop thinking for a while. Four is stability. Swords is thought. Stable thought is meditation — the mind at rest but not asleep. Your brain has been running at full speed. The arguments cycling, the decisions unmade, the problems unsolved. They're all still there, hanging on the wall like the three swords. But you've set them aside. Not solved. Not forgotten. Just paused. The knight's sword beneath him means he hasn't given up. He's recharging. There's a stained glass window in this card showing a figure receiving a blessing. Rest isn't laziness. It's the part of the process where the subconscious does the heavy lifting your conscious mind couldn't. Scientists have known for decades that insight arrives during rest, not effort. The mind solves problems while you sleep. When this card appears, the best move is to stop moving. Not permanently. Not even for long. Just long enough for the mental noise to settle and the signal to emerge from the static. You've been thinking hard. The answer isn't more thinking. The answer is the space between thoughts.
