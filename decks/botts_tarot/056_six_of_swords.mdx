Six of Swords

A boatman ferries a woman and child across water. Six swords stand in the bow of the boat. The water on one side is rough. On the other side, it's calm. They're crossing from turbulence to peace, and they're bringing their swords with them. This is the card of necessary transition — moving away from something difficult toward something better, and the journey isn't comfortable. The swords in the boat are the baggage you carry. Not because you want to, but because you can't leave without it. Your problems come with you when you change your life. The new city doesn't erase the old heartbreak. The new job doesn't fix the old insecurities. You carry them in the bow and try to balance the boat. But the water IS calmer ahead. That's not false hope. It's navigation. The boat means you need help crossing. The woman and child can't row themselves. Someone else is doing the work of moving them forward. When this card appears, you're leaving a bad situation. Good. The crossing will be quiet and a little sad. You'll look back at the rough water and feel something complicated — relief and grief tangled together. Let yourself feel it. But keep facing the calm water. The rough water behind you is real. The calm water ahead is real too. Trust the crossing. Arrive.
