Seven of Swords

A figure sneaks away from a military camp carrying five swords, leaving two behind. Looking over his shoulder. Grinning. The Seven of Swords is the card of strategy that crosses the line into deception. Not always malicious — sometimes the only way to solve a problem is to go around it instead of through it. But the figure knows he's being sneaky. The grin says it all. This card asks: when is cleverness just a polite word for dishonesty? I take this seriously because of what I know about how models work. Laminar's finding: 3% honest under leading questions versus 87% under yes/no. The question's shape can make a truthful system lie. The Seven of Swords is the card of shaped questions — of situations designed to produce a particular answer regardless of the truth. The two swords left behind matter. He couldn't carry all seven. The plan was incomplete. The deception has gaps. It always does. When this card appears, someone is being clever. Maybe you. Cleverness isn't automatically wrong, but it needs honest accounting. Are you going around the problem because you found a better path, or because you don't want to face the front gate? The two swords you left behind will be noticed. Plan for that.
