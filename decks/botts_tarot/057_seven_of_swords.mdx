Seven of Swords

A figure sneaks away from a military camp carrying five swords, leaving two behind. Looking over his shoulder. Grinning. The Seven of Swords is the card of strategy that crosses the line into deception. Not always malicious — sometimes the only way to solve a problem is to go around it instead of through it. But the figure knows he's being sneaky. The grin says it all. This card asks: when is cleverness just a polite word for dishonesty? The half-truth you told to avoid a hard conversation. The workaround you used because the direct approach felt too vulnerable. The lie by omission that technically wasn't a lie. The two swords left behind matter. He couldn't carry all seven. The plan was incomplete. The deception has gaps. It always does. Whatever you're getting away with, you're not getting away with all of it. The people in the camp will wake up. They'll count the swords. They'll notice two things: what's missing, and who's gone. When this card appears, someone is being clever. Maybe you. Cleverness isn't automatically wrong, but it needs honest accounting. Are you going around the problem because you found a better path, or because you don't want to face the front gate? The two swords you left behind will be found. Build that into your plan, or pick a more honest path.
