Nine of Swords

A figure sits up in bed, face in hands. Nine swords hang on the wall behind them in the dark. The nightmare card. Not because something terrible is happening — because something terrible might happen, and your mind has decided to rehearse it at 3 AM. The Nine of Swords is anxiety: the mind's sword turned against itself, cutting into things that haven't happened yet. The phone call you're dreading. The test results you haven't gotten. The conversation you keep rehearsing with someone who isn't in the room. Nine swords on the wall, not in your body. The pain is mental, not physical. Which doesn't make it less real. Just differently addressable. The quilt on the bed shows roses and astrological symbols — beauty woven into the fabric of the suffering. Even in the nightmare, the life you've built is still there. The bed is warm. The pillow is soft. The swords are on the wall, not in your chest. Your mind is showing you a horror movie and you've forgotten you're in the theater. When this card appears, the fear is disproportionate to the actual threat. That doesn't mean the fear isn't real. It means the thing you're afraid of probably isn't as bad as 3 AM is making it look. Get out of bed. Turn on the light. The swords are smaller than they looked in the dark. They always are.
