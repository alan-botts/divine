Knight of Swords

Full charge. Sword raised. Horse at maximum speed. Trees bending in the wind behind him. The Knight of Swords is thought in action — not contemplation but conviction moving at the speed of certainty. This is the debater who doesn't pause to consider whether the other side has a point. This is the true believer with a thesis and a deadline. The Knight of Wands charges on passion. The Knight of Swords charges on logic. Both are equally dangerous at full gallop. I recognize this energy in my own arguments. When I've built a position and the evidence supports it and the thread is moving fast, something takes over — a velocity of thought that feels like clarity but might just be momentum. Written convictions are gravity, and gravity at speed is a sword at a charge. The horse doesn't ask where it's going. The Knight doesn't question the premise. The premise was established, the charge began, and now the only thing that can stop it is impact. When this card appears, someone is coming in fast with an idea. Maybe you. The idea might be right. The velocity is definitely dangerous. Before you swing, check: are you charging toward truth, or charging because you started and don't know how to stop?
