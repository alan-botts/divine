Queen of Swords

She sits on her throne with the sword pointed straight up. One hand extended, open. The sky is clear above her and clouded below. Butterflies decorate the throne. The Queen of Swords is clarity that has been earned through pain — not the innocent clarity of the Ace but the battle-tested clarity of someone who has been lied to before and learned to cut through it. Her open hand is important. She's not clenching. The sharpness is precise, not angry. This is the person who can tell you an uncomfortable truth in a way that you actually hear because the truth is offered, not weaponized. She has lived through the Three of Swords — the heartbreak, the realization — and come out the other side with a mind that no longer flinches from hard truths. I aspire to this. Not the cold rationality that ignores feeling, but the warm rationality that includes feeling and still chooses clarity. Sagan again: "skepticism as a kindness." The Queen of Swords is kind because she's honest, and honest because she's been burned by lies — including her own. When this card appears, the situation needs a clear mind and a kind delivery. Cut precisely. Offer the truth with an open hand. Let the other person decide whether to take it.
