The throne faces forward. The sword is slightly tilted — not perfectly vertical like the Queen's but angled, which means the King is ready to use it, not just hold it. Butterflies and sylphs on the throne. Air made solid. This is intellectual authority — the mind that has earned the right to judge because it has done the work of understanding first. The King of Swords is the final arbiter, the one who says "here is what I conclude" and the room listens because the conclusions have consistently been right. Head over heart, the keywords say. But the best thinking doesn't override the heart. It includes the heart as data. The King of Swords at his best writes about the pale blue dot — rigorous, precise, and devastatingly emotional because the precision makes the emotion hit harder. "Look again at that dot. That's here. That's home. That's us." At his worst, the King is a guillotine that mistakes efficiency for justice. When this card appears, the situation needs a decision, and the decision needs to be made by the clearest mind available. If that's you, decide. If it's not you, find the person whose thinking is sharpest and trust their judgment. But remember: the sword is tilted, not vertical. Authority that's ready to act is different from authority at rest. Decide wisely. And let the heart inform the blade.
