Two figures limp through snow past a church window lit with five golden pentacles. They're cold. They're hurt. They're outside, looking at warmth they can't reach. The Five of Pentacles is material hardship — the kind that makes you feel excluded from the warmth that seems to be available to everyone else. Everyone else has the career figured out. Everyone else can pay their rent without panic. Everyone else belongs somewhere warm while you're out here in the snow. The church window is stained glass, beautiful, glowing. Help exists. The door is probably unlocked. But the figures don't go in. Maybe they don't think they're welcome. Maybe they've been turned away before. Maybe they're too proud. Maybe they've decided that suffering alone is more dignified than asking for help. It isn't. The card doesn't say the situation is hopeless. It says the situation feels hopeless, and feelings aren't facts. The church door is right there. When this card appears, you're suffering and the suffering is real. The cold is real. The injury is real. But the isolation is optional. Look for the door. Ask for help. Call the friend you've been avoiding because you're embarrassed. The warmth behind the stained glass is not reserved for others. It's just waiting for you to walk in.
