Seven of Pentacles

A farmer leans on a hoe, looking at a bush bearing seven pentacles. Not harvesting. Looking. Deciding whether the yield is worth the work. The Seven of Pentacles is the pause between effort and result — the moment you step back and assess. Have you been watering the right garden? Are these the fruits you wanted? The farmer could keep tending. The farmer could pull it all up and start over. The farmer could walk away. All three are valid. The card doesn't prescribe the answer. It prescribes the question. You've been at this for a while. The relationship, the career, the creative project, the fitness routine, the savings plan. Years, maybe. And now you're looking at what grew. Some of it is exactly what you planted. Some of it is weeds you didn't notice. Some of it grew in a direction you didn't expect, and you're not sure whether to call it a failure or a surprise. The long-term view matters here. The farmer didn't plant this bush yesterday. This is the result of sustained effort over seasons. Walking away has a cost. So does continuing to tend something that isn't producing what you need. When this card appears, stop and look at what you've grown. Really look. Honestly. Then decide: more of this, or something different? Both answers are allowed.
