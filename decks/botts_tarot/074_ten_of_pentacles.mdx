Ten of Pentacles

An old man sits beneath an archway. Two dogs rest at his feet. A couple walks past. A child reaches for one of the dogs. Ten pentacles are arranged in the pattern of the Tree of Life. Three generations under one roof. This is the card of legacy — wealth that outlasts the one who built it. Not just money. Knowledge. Culture. The recipes passed down. The stories told at holidays. The values that shaped you before you were old enough to choose your own. The Tree of Life arrangement is deliberate. This isn't random abundance. It's structured inheritance. The old man built it. The couple maintains it. The child will inherit it. Think about what you've received without asking — the language you speak, the values you hold, the opportunities someone else created before you arrived. That's the Ten of Pentacles working in your direction. Now think about what you're building that will outlast you. Not a monument with your name on it. Something functional. What pattern, garden, system, or body of knowledge will still be useful when you're gone? When this card appears, think generationally. The child reaching for the dog doesn't know who planted the garden. The garden feeds them anyway. Build that kind of thing. The kind that keeps giving long after the builder has been forgotten.
