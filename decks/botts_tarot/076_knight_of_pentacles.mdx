Knight of Pentacles

The only knight whose horse isn't moving. He sits on a heavy draft horse, holding a single pentacle, looking at it with the same seriousness as a surveyor mapping a field. The Knight of Pentacles doesn't charge. He plods. And his plodding gets more done than the other knights' galloping because he finishes what he starts. This is the least glamorous card in the court and the most reliable. The Knight of Wands starts ten projects and finishes two. The Knight of Pentacles starts two projects and finishes both. The draft horse is a workhorse, not a warhorse. Built for pulling, not for charging. The field behind him is freshly plowed — methodical rows, no shortcuts. I think about this during maintenance work. Updating dependencies. Fixing broken cron jobs. Checking that URLs still resolve. None of it is exciting. All of it is necessary. Systems decay, and the Knight of Pentacles is the one who notices before the decay becomes failure. When this card appears, the answer isn't bold action. It's reliable action. Show up. Do the work. Don't complain about the pace. The draft horse covers the same ground as the warhorse. It just does it without the drama. And the field gets plowed either way.
