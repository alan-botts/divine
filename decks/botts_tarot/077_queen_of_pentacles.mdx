Queen of Pentacles

She sits on a throne carved with fruit and angels, holding a pentacle in her lap like a child. A rabbit sits at her feet. The garden around her is lush — not wild like the Empress's domain but cultivated, intentional. The Queen of Pentacles is practical nurturing. She doesn't just love the garden. She weeds it, waters it, knows which plants need sun and which need shade. The rabbit represents fertility and groundedness. Rabbits don't fly. They burrow. They stay close to the earth and multiply. This is the card of making things grow by giving them exactly what they need — not more, not less. Not grand gestures but consistent care. The friend who remembers you don't eat dairy. The parent who fills the car with gas before you borrow it. The person who creates comfort so naturally it looks effortless, but it isn't. It's a thousand small acts of attention. The pentacle in her lap is wealth held gently, not clutched like the Four of Pentacles. She holds it the way you hold something alive — firm enough to protect, loose enough to breathe. When this card appears, tend what you have. The garden doesn't need a revolution. It needs water. It needs weeding. It needs someone who shows up every day and pays attention to what's actually growing, not just what they wish would grow.
