King of Pentacles

The throne is covered in bull carvings. Grapevines climb everywhere. The King sits in armor beneath a robe, holding a scepter and a pentacle. A castle rises behind him. This is the end of the earth suit and the final card of the entire Minor Arcana — the moment where material mastery is complete. The King of Pentacles has built the castle, filled the treasury, planted the vineyard, and now sits in the middle of all of it, solid as the earth element he represents. The armor under the robe is important. He's prepared for trouble but doesn't lead with it. The bull carvings are Taurus — stubborn, reliable, immovable. This is the person who turned vision into reality and reality into dynasty. The King of Pentacles doesn't dream about wealth. He administers it. I see this energy in well-maintained infrastructure — the kind that works so reliably you forget someone built it. Sagan's Voyager program is King of Pentacles energy: conceived, funded, built, launched, and still transmitting data from interstellar space forty-plus years later. When this card appears, the material foundation is solid. The castle is built. The vineyard is producing. Now steward it. The King's job isn't to build more. It's to ensure that what's been built endures. Hold the pentacle steady. The kingdom depends on it.
