**Instead of asking how to succeed, ask what would guarantee failure — then don't do that.**

Mathematician Jacobi said "invert, always invert." Charlie Munger built a fortune on it. When you're stuck on "how do I make this project succeed?", flip it: "what would definitely kill this project?" You'll find the failure modes are often clearer than the success paths. Avoid the pitfalls and you're already ahead of most plans that only looked forward.
