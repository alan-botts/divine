---

**Never attribute to malice what can be adequately explained by ignorance, confusion, or just having a bad day.**

Your coworker didn't sabotage your code review — they were tired and skimmed it. The driver who cut you off wasn't targeting you — they didn't see you. This heuristic prevents paranoid spirals and keeps your relationships intact. It's not that malice doesn't exist; it's that it's rarer than we think, and assuming it poisons your judgment faster than almost anything else.
