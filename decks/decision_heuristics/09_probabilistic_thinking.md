**Think in likelihoods, not certainties. Assign rough odds and update them as you learn.**

The world doesn't deal in yes or no — it deals in 70/30 and 90/10. When someone says "this will definitely work," translate that into a probability. How confident are you, really? 60%? 85%? This practice alone prevents overconfidence and makes you better at allocating resources. You don't need to be a mathematician. You just need to stop pretending you're certain when you're not.
