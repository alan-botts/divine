**Start with a prior belief, then update it proportionally when new evidence arrives.**

You think there's a 30% chance a candidate will work out. They crush the technical interview — update to 50%. Their references are lukewarm — adjust back to 40%. Bayesian thinking is just disciplined belief revision: each new piece of evidence should move your estimate, but not blow it up entirely. The key insight is that your starting estimate matters, and extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence to shift it.
