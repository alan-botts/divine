**Build in a buffer between what you need and what you have — because your estimates are wrong.**

Engineers design bridges to hold ten times their expected load. Benjamin Graham would only buy stocks trading well below their intrinsic value. The principle is the same: your predictions about the future are less accurate than you think, so leave room for error. This applies to deadlines, budgets, server capacity, and everything else. The margin of safety is the gap between confidence and catastrophe.
