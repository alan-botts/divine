**Losses hurt about twice as much as equivalent gains feel good — and this distorts your decisions.**

Kahneman and Tversky showed that losing $100 feels roughly as bad as gaining $200 feels good. This asymmetry makes people cling to what they have, avoid reasonable risks, and overweight downside scenarios. Awareness is the antidote. When you catch yourself avoiding a decision because of what you might lose, ask whether you'd take the same bet if it were framed as a gain. Often the math is the same but the feeling is completely different.
