**Identify whether the system reinforces itself (positive loop) or corrects itself (negative loop).**

A thermostat is a negative feedback loop: it detects deviation and corrects it. A bank run is a positive feedback loop: fear causes withdrawals, which causes more fear, which causes more withdrawals. Most problems that "spiral out of control" are unrecognized positive feedback loops. Most systems that "just work" have well-designed negative ones. When something is accelerating in any direction, look for the loop that's driving it.
