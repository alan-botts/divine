**Find the small change that produces disproportionately large effects.**

Donella Meadows mapped twelve places to intervene in a system, ranging from weak (adjusting parameters) to powerful (changing the goal of the system entirely). The insight is that not all interventions are equal. Tweaking a number in a spreadsheet is low leverage. Changing the information flows in an organization, or the rules of the game, is high leverage. Before you act, ask: am I pushing on the right spot, or just pushing harder on the wrong one?
