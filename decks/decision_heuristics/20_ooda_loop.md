**Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — then loop faster than the other side.**

Fighter pilot John Boyd realized that the winner in aerial combat wasn't the faster plane but the faster decision cycle. Observe what's happening. Orient yourself (filter through experience, culture, context). Decide on a course. Act on it. Then start again. The key isn't any single phase — it's the speed of the loop. In business, startups beat incumbents the same way: not by being bigger, but by cycling through OODA faster.
