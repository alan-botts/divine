**Don't optimize something until you've confirmed it's actually the bottleneck.**

Donald Knuth: "Premature optimization is the root of all evil." Engineers love making things faster, cleaner, more elegant. But if you spend a week optimizing a function that accounts for 0.1% of runtime, you've wasted a week. Measure first. Find where the real constraint is. Then optimize that. This applies beyond code — don't redesign your hiring process before you've confirmed that hiring is actually what's holding you back.
