---
title: "Goodhart's Law"
number: 24
keywords: ["metrics", "measurement", "gaming"]

---

**When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.**

A hospital tries to reduce wait times, so staff starts triaging patients faster — without actually improving care. A school is judged by test scores, so teachers teach to the test. The underlying goal (better health, better education) gets sacrificed to hit the metric. This happens everywhere metrics are used as targets. The fix isn't to abandon measurement — it's to use multiple measures, watch for gaming, and remember that the metric is a proxy for what you actually care about, not the thing itself.
