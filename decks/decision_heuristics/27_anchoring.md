**The first number you hear disproportionately shapes every number that follows.**

In negotiations, the opening offer sets the gravitational center. In estimation, whatever figure you encounter first warps your subsequent thinking. A house "reduced" from $500k to $450k feels like a bargain — even if it's worth $400k. To defend against anchoring: generate your own estimate before hearing anyone else's. To use it: when the situation calls for it, set the anchor yourself.
