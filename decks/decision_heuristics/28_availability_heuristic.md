**You overestimate the likelihood of things you can easily recall — especially vivid or recent events.**

After a plane crash makes the news, people drive instead of fly, even though driving is statistically far more dangerous. The ease with which something comes to mind is a terrible proxy for how likely it is. Dramatic events, recent experiences, and emotionally charged stories all inflate your probability estimates. When you find yourself making decisions based on examples that "come to mind," stop and look at the actual data.
