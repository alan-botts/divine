**Extreme results tend to be followed by more ordinary ones. Don't overreact to outliers.**

A team has an incredible quarter. You promote the manager and replicate their process across the org. Next quarter: back to normal. Was the manager a fraud? No — the first quarter was partly luck, and luck doesn't repeat reliably. Regression to the mean explains why the "Sports Illustrated cover jinx" exists, why miracle cures seem to work (people seek treatment at their worst), and why you shouldn't restructure your entire strategy based on one data point.
