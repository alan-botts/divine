**In many domains, a tiny number of things account for the vast majority of outcomes.**

One hit song pays for a hundred failures. One viral post drives more traffic than the last thousand combined. One investment returns more than the rest of the portfolio. Power law distributions are everywhere — city sizes, earthquake magnitudes, startup returns — and they violate our intuition, which expects bell curves. The strategic implication: spend time finding the rare outlier that matters, rather than optimizing across the average.
