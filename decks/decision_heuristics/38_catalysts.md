**Some things don't cause change directly — they lower the barrier so change can happen.**

An enzyme doesn't force a reaction; it makes the reaction easier. A great manager doesn't do the work; they remove obstacles so the team can. A well-written spec doesn't build the feature; it makes building possible. When you want to accelerate change, ask: "What's blocking this from happening naturally?" Then remove the blocker rather than pushing harder. Catalytic leadership is about clearing the path, not dragging people down it.
