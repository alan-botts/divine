**You might be at the top of the wrong hill. Sometimes you have to go down before you can climb higher.**

Imagine walking uphill in fog. You reach a peak — but it's a small hill, not the mountain. To reach the real summit, you'd have to descend first. This is the local maximum trap: optimizing your current approach feels like progress but might prevent you from finding a fundamentally better one. Career changes, product pivots, and technology migrations all require temporarily getting worse to get much better. The question is: are you climbing the right hill?
