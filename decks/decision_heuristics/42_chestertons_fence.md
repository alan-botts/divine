**Before you tear something down, understand why it was built in the first place.**

G.K. Chesterton imagined a fence across a road. A reformer says "I don't see the use of this; let's tear it down." Chesterton replies: "If you don't see the use, I won't let you remove it. Go away and think. When you can tell me why it's here, I'll let you decide whether to keep it." Every "legacy" system, every "pointless" process, every "stupid" rule was put there by someone solving a problem you might not see yet. Understand the problem first, then decide.
