**The longer something has survived, the longer it's likely to continue surviving.**

A book that's been in print for fifty years will probably be in print for another fifty. A restaurant that's been open for twenty years will probably outlast one that opened last month. The Lindy Effect applies to ideas, technologies, and institutions — things that are "antifragile" gain from time. When choosing tools, frameworks, or strategies, longevity is a powerful signal. New isn't always better. Sometimes the old thing is old precisely because it works.
