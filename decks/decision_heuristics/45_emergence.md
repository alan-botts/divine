**Complex behavior arises from simple rules interacting. You can't always predict the whole from the parts.**

Ants following three simple rules create colonies of staggering complexity. Traders following individual self-interest create market crashes no one intended. Conway's Game of Life generates infinite complexity from four rules. The lesson: when you're designing systems with many interacting agents — organizations, markets, software — the aggregate behavior will surprise you. You can't fully predict emergent behavior, but you can create conditions where good emergence is more likely than bad.
