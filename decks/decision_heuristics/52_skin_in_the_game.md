**Don't trust advice from people who don't bear the consequences of being wrong.**

Nassim Taleb's central insight: symmetry between risk and reward is the foundation of sound decision-making. A financial advisor who doesn't invest their own money in what they recommend has no skin in the game. A pundit who faces no consequence for wrong predictions will make bold, reckless ones. When evaluating advice, always ask: "What happens to this person if they're wrong?" If the answer is "nothing," weight their opinion accordingly.
