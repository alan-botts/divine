New territories are inherently unstable because the people who helped you gain power will soon expect more than you can deliver, while those you displaced will never stop resenting you. The prince who acquires new lands must recognize that the act of conquest is only the beginning of a far harder struggle.

— Chapter I-II: Types of Principalities
