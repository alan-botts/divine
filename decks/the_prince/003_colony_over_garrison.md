Colonies cost almost nothing and offend only a few displaced people who are too scattered and poor to cause trouble. A military garrison, by contrast, bleeds your treasury and offends everyone. When the choice is between a small injustice to a few and a ruinous expense that angers many, choose the colony.

— Chapter III: Mixed Principalities
