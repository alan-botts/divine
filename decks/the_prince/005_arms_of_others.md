Those who rise to power on the strength of other people's armies or fortunes find it easy to acquire but impossible to maintain. They are at the mercy of the will and fortune of whoever elevated them. A throne built on borrowed power has no foundation of its own.

— Chapter VII: New Principalities Acquired by Others' Arms
