Cesare Borgia did everything a prudent and capable man should do to secure his power: he destroyed rival families, won over their followers, built his own army, and neutralized threats through a mix of force and cunning. His only mistake was failing to prevent a hostile pope — proof that even the most thorough plans cannot fully account for fate.
