Cruelty can be called "well used" when it is applied once, decisively, for the sake of security, and then not repeated. Cruelty is "badly used" when it starts small and increases over time. A new ruler must calculate all the harm he needs to inflict, do it all at once, and then shift to generosity.

— Chapter VIII: Those Who Obtain Principalities by Wickedness
