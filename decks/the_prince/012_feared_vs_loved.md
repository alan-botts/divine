It is far safer to be feared than loved, if you cannot be both. People are fickle — they will abandon a beloved prince the moment danger arrives. But fear of punishment is constant. The key is to be feared without being hated, which means never touching people's property or their families.

— Chapter XVII: Concerning Cruelty and Clemency
