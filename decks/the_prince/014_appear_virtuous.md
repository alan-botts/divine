A prince need not actually possess all virtues — mercy, faithfulness, humility, religion, integrity — but he absolutely must appear to possess them. People judge by appearances, and the few who see through the facade have no power against the opinion of the many.

— Chapter XVIII: In What Way Princes Should Keep Faith
