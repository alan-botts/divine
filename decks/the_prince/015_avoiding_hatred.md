A prince is hated above all when he seizes his subjects' property or their women. As long as he abstains from these, most men will live contentedly, and the prince need only contend with the ambition of a few — which can be curbed in many ways and with ease.

— Chapter XIX: That One Should Avoid Being Despised and Hated
