title: "Avoiding Contempt"
number: 16

---

A prince earns contempt by being seen as fickle, frivolous, effeminate, cowardly, or irresolute. He should strive for greatness, spirit, gravity, and strength in every action. Against his subjects' private dealings he should be immovable in judgment, maintaining a reputation that discourages any thought of deceiving or manipulating him.

— Chapter XIX: That One Should Avoid Being Despised and Hated
