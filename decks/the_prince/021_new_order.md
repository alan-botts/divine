There is nothing more difficult to take in hand, more perilous to conduct, or more uncertain in its success than introducing a new order of things. The innovator has for enemies all who prospered under the old system and only lukewarm defenders among those who might benefit from the new.

— Chapter VI: New Principalities Acquired by One's Own Arms
