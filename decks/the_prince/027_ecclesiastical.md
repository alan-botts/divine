Church-based states are the only ones that are secure without either good arms or good leadership. They are sustained by ancient religious customs so powerful that the princes remain in power regardless of how they live or act. They alone have states and do not defend them, have subjects and do not govern them — and yet no one complains.

— Chapter XI: Concerning Ecclesiastical Principalities
