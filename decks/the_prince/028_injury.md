Injuries should be done all at once, so that being less tasted, they offend less. Benefits should be given little by little, so that they may be better savored. A ruler must above all live with his subjects so that no event — whether good or bad — forces him to change course.

— Chapter VIII: Those Who Obtain Principalities by Wickedness
