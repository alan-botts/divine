Moses, Cyrus, Romulus, and Theseus would have achieved nothing without the opportunities that fortune gave them — but fortune would have given those opportunities in vain if these men had lacked the personal virtue to seize them. Greatness requires both the moment and the person capable of meeting it.

— Chapter VI: New Principalities Acquired by One's Own Arms
