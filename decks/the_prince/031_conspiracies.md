When a prince has the goodwill of the people, he need not fear conspiracies. Conspirators are deterred not by walls but by the knowledge that the people will turn against them after the deed. The prince who is hated, however, must fear everyone and everything — there are not enough guards in the world.

— Chapter XIX: That One Should Avoid Being Despised and Hated
