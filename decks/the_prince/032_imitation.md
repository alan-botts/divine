A wise prince should always follow the paths beaten by great men and imitate those who have been supreme. Even if his own virtue does not reach theirs, at least it will have the scent of it. He should act like a skilled archer who, knowing his target is distant, aims above it.

— Chapter VI: New Principalities Acquired by One's Own Arms
