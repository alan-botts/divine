Everyone sees what you appear to be; few perceive what you are. A prince who has known how to use the fox's nature has fared best. But it is necessary to know how to disguise this nature well, and to be a great pretender and dissembler. Those who have been the best at deception have overcome those who relied on honesty.

— Chapter XVIII: In What Way Princes Should Keep Faith
