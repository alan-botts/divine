A prince can compose his army of his own soldiers mixed with mercenaries or auxiliaries, and this is far better than relying on auxiliaries or mercenaries alone. But even a mixed force is inferior to one's own troops. The lesson of history is clear: no state is secure unless it has its own arms.

— Chapter XIII: Concerning Auxiliaries, Mixed Soldiery, and One's Own
