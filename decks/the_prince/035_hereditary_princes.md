Hereditary states accustomed to a prince's family are far easier to hold than new ones. As long as the hereditary prince does not provoke his subjects with extraordinary vices, and as long as he simply follows long-standing customs, he will maintain his position even through setbacks. Time and familiarity are powerful stabilizers.

— Chapter II: Concerning Hereditary Principalities
