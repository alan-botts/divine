A new prince cannot avoid a reputation for cruelty, because new states are full of dangers. Nevertheless, he should be cautious in believing and acting, not afraid of his own shadow, and proceed in a temperate manner with prudence and humanity. Excessive mercy that permits disorders harms the whole community, while targeted severity corrects a few to set an example for all.

— Chapter XVII: Concerning Cruelty and Clemency
