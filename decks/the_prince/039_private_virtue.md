Some qualities that appear virtuous will lead a prince to ruin, and some that appear vicious will bring him security and prosperity. The prince must not care about the reproach of those vices without which it would be difficult to save the state. Judging actions by their outcomes rather than their morality is the realist's burden.

— Chapter XV: Concerning Things for Which Princes Are Praised or Blamed
