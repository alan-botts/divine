Machiavelli's final chapter is an impassioned plea for a new prince to liberate Italy from foreign domination. The raw materials exist — brave people, a willing populace, a desperate need — but they lack a leader to organize them. In every great endeavor, the moment and the individual must align. This is that moment; who will be that individual?

— Chapter XXVI: An Exhortation to Liberate Italy
