When you injure a man, do it so severely that you need not fear his revenge. Half-measures are the greatest danger in politics. Either treat conquered peoples well enough that they have no cause for grievance, or crush them so thoroughly that they cannot retaliate. The middle path — moderate harm — creates enemies who are both wounded and capable.

— Chapter III: Mixed Principalities
