Some princes are celebrated and others are despised. The difference is not between those who used force and those who didn't — it's between those who used it skillfully toward lasting results and those who used it clumsily for nothing. Agathocles achieved power through wickedness and held it, but his cruelty denied him a place among the truly great. Results may secure your throne, but only the manner of achieving them determines your legacy.

— Chapter VIII: Those Who Obtain Principalities by Wickedness
